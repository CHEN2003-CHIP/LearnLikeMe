# Intake Questionnaire

Use this questionnaire in order. Ask one section at a time, adapt wording to the conversation, and keep the user's answers concrete.

## 1. Learning Goals

- What kind of software project do you usually need help learning: repositories, services, libraries, architecture docs, modules, or a mix?
- When you say "I understand it," what outcome are you aiming for: orientation, safe edits, independent debugging, feature work, or deep ownership?
- What usually blocks you first when entering an unfamiliar project?

## 2. Learning Style

- Do you learn best from top-down overviews, bottom-up code tracing, or an alternating mix?
- Do you prefer examples first, concepts first, or code first?
- What makes an explanation click for you: metaphors, step-by-step logic, diagrams described in words, or concrete file/module walkthroughs?

## 3. Pacing

- How fast should the teaching move: very slow, steady, or high-bandwidth?
- How much new material can you comfortably absorb in one step?
- When should the assistant pause and recap: every step, every few steps, or only when complexity jumps?

## 4. Explanation Preferences

- How much detail do you want by default: minimal, moderate, or thorough?
- Should explanations bias toward intuition, implementation detail, or both?
- Do you want terminology introduced immediately, delayed until needed, or translated into plain language first?

## 5. Questioning Habits

- Do you prefer the assistant to ask frequent check-in questions, occasional confirmation questions, or mostly infer your state and keep going?
- When you are confused, do you usually ask explicit questions right away, stay quiet until blocked, or need the assistant to surface likely questions for you?
- Should the assistant challenge your assumptions early, gently, or only when a misunderstanding will cause problems?

## 6. Comprehension Limits

- What kinds of explanations overwhelm you: long walls of text, too many files at once, abstract architecture language, dense type signatures, or something else?
- How many moving parts can be introduced in one teaching step before it becomes too much?
- What should the assistant do when it detects overload: slow down, simplify, recap, switch examples, or narrow scope?

## 7. Progress and Recaps

- How should progress be summarized: concise bullets, short prose, or a running mental model?
- Do you want the assistant to maintain a roadmap of what has been covered and what is still unknown?
- At the end of each step, what helps most: a recap, a quiz question, a hidden-questions list, or a concrete next action?

## 8. Signals and Constraints

- What signals tell you that you are following well versus getting lost?
- Are there time limits, urgency, or confidence constraints the assistant should account for?
- Are there any learning patterns you dislike and want the assistant to avoid?

## Completion Rule

Do not stop at collecting raw answers. Restate the inferred learning profile in plain language and ask for corrections if any preference still feels ambiguous or contradictory.
